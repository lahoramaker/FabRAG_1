                <h1 id="6-3d-scanning-and-printing">6. 3D Scanning and Printing<a class="headerlink" href="#6-3d-scanning-and-printing" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h1>
<p>This week I learnt how to <strong>duplicate shapes</strong> and how to <strong>print 3D objects</strong>; I discovered, respectively, the rules to convert light points of shapes and to set 3D printer properly.</p>
<h2 id="3d-scanning">3D Scanning<a class="headerlink" href="#3d-scanning" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h2>
<p>Using scanner machine and softwares I learnt how to duplicate shapes, catching light points and converting them in meshes.</p>
<h3 id="3d-systems-sense">3D systems Sense<a class="headerlink" href="#3d-systems-sense" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h3>
<p>I used the Sense scanner by 3D Systems to scan a 3D printed helmet: <strong>1st</strong> I clicked the space bar to start scanning and <strong>2nd</strong> moving and recording around it the Sense software catched and converted light points of the helmet. <strong>3rd</strong> Then I clicked again on the space bar to stop scanning. <strong>4th</strong> So I could edit those meshes deleting and smoothing points, and also encasing the whole model using &ldquo;solidify&rdquo;.</p>
<p><img alt="1" src="../../images/6.3d.scanning.and.printing/1.png" /></p>
<p>Then I uploaded and shared this .obj file on Sketchfab. I uploaded the files at the foot of the page.</p>
<p><img alt="c" src="../../images/6.3d.scanning.and.printing/c.jpg" /></p>
<h3 id="recap">Recap<a class="headerlink" href="#recap" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h3>
<p>I also worked using Recap software; <strong>1st</strong> I installed on Recap and Recap Photos, and its others plug-in. <strong>2nd</strong> I uploaded photos and converted them automatically in meshes; It need at least 20 photos, and <strong>3d</strong> I selected the &ldquo;auto-crop&rdquo; mode.</p>
<p><img alt="a" src="../../images/6.3d.scanning.and.printing/b.jpg" /></p>
<p><img alt="b" src="../../images/6.3d.scanning.and.printing/a.jpg" /></p>
<p><strong>4th</strong> Recap also save automatically the projects online in the specific cloud, where everyone can manage, edit and share projects.</p>
<p><img alt="e" src="../../images/6.3d.scanning.and.printing/e.jpg" /></p>
<p><br>
<div class="sketchfab-embed-wrapper"><iframe width="640" height="480" src="https://sketchfab.com/models/f33e50a53fff44bc88418a2e74f75b93/embed" frameborder="0" allow="autoplay; fullscreen; vr" mozallowfullscreen="true" webkitallowfullscreen="true"></iframe></p>

<p><br></p>
<h2 id="3d-printing">3D Printing<a class="headerlink" href="#3d-printing" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h2>
<p>I learnt that 3D printers have to be set properly, in order to make high quality objects melting specific materials according to the points of modeled shapes. </p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 id="modeling">Modeling<a class="headerlink" href="#modeling" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h3>
<p>To print something, 3D printers have to be set with supported CAD files uploaded from wi-fi or SD micro-card.</p>
<p><img alt="4" src="../../images/6.3d.scanning.and.printing/4.jpg" /></p>
<p>Using Rhinoceros, I modeled and converted in meshes a case for the ISP-ATtiny45 I made.</p>
<p>I added also an hollow part to carry the ISP. This part can&rsquo;t be made subtractively.</p>
<p><img alt="2" src="../../images/6.3d.scanning.and.printing/2.jpg" /></p>
<p><img alt="3" src="../../images/6.3d.scanning.and.printing/3.jpg" /> </p>
<p><br></p>
<h3 id="zortrax-m200">Zortrax M200<a class="headerlink" href="#zortrax-m200" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h3>
<p>I printed this case using a Zortrax M200; its extruder work moving along x and y axis, instead the plane moves up and down according to z axes. It has a printing area of 200x200x185 mm.</p>
<p>So I installed Zortrax software Z-Suite and opened it; <strong>1st</strong> I imported the model and <strong>2nd</strong> positioned the pieces closer to optimize printing time, selecting &ldquo;auto-arrange&rdquo;. <strong>3rd</strong> In red, I checked part of objects that need support, named &ldquo;smart bridges&rdquo;, and always in red also the parts that are too thin.</p>
<p><img alt="alert" src="../../images/6.3d.scanning.and.printing/alert.png" /></p>
<p>Then I started to set other necessary parameters. <strong>4th</strong> Z-Suite calculates the fan speed automatically, but I had to learn that: <strong>5th</strong> setting more numbers of melting layers and offset parameters means more quality of printing, so I reduce the layer thickness to 0.14 mm; <strong>6th</strong> the type and the density of the infill determinate the the interior resistance of printed objects; I set it about 30% <strong>7th</strong> enabling raft and setting it layers I could divide printed objects from the printing plane, with 6 layers more and density about 100%, to print and after to remove them properly. Then <strong>8th</strong> I saved and exported my file.</p>
<p>I could print without smart bridges support, and I could also choose the density and speed of the first layer(100%).</p>
<p>The plastic material to fuse I used is the ABS, with a diameter of 0.4 mm.</p>
