                <h1 id="8-computer-controlled-machining">8. Computer controlled machining<a class="headerlink" href="#8-computer-controlled-machining" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h1>
<h2 id="cad-design">CAD Design<a class="headerlink" href="#cad-design" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h2>
<h3 id="parts-layout">Parts layout<a class="headerlink" href="#parts-layout" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h3>
<p><img alt="" src="../../images/danpuzzle.png" />
I first wanted to make a smaller scale prototype on the laser cutter to test for fit and assembly.</p>
<h2 id="laser-cut-prototype">Laser Cut Prototype<a class="headerlink" href="#laser-cut-prototype" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h2>
<p><img alt="" src="../../images/puzzleoutside.jpg" /></p>
<p><img alt="" src="../../images/puzzleinside.jpg" /></p>
<p><img alt="" src="../../images/crazy-cube-scales.jpg" />
Now&hellip;to scale it up!</p>
<h2 id="shopbot-cnc-toolpathing-downloadable-files">Shopbot CNC Toolpathing - downloadable files<a class="headerlink" href="#shopbot-cnc-toolpathing-downloadable-files" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h2>
<p>First I remade the parts layout to reflect needed changes for the larger scale cnc cut puzzle.
<img alt="" src="../../images/4x8layout.PNG" /></p>
<p>I used ShopBot&rsquo;s VCarve program to define the tool paths - all path were set to the Contour method.</p>
<p>Includes: I scaled overall 2x the original size. I added dog bone corners, and I made the parts 8X thicker so that they&rsquo;d be more chunky at larger scale as opposed to planar.</p>
<p><a href="../../images/puzzle tool path prep.3dm">Rhino parts layout download files</a></p>
<h2 id="2d-workflow">2D Workflow<a class="headerlink" href="#2d-workflow" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h2>
<p>Here&rsquo;s a Rhino screenshot to illustrate a 2d workflow for modifying the red geometry to be ready for milling, the black geometry. 
<img alt="" src="../../images/fillets2.png" /></p>
<p>The main 2D drafting operations I used were fillet, offset, move, rotate, and join. By using thicker material I need to offset the lines to make the gap wider. I also need to account interior corners when using press fit/slotted geometry, hence the dogbone geometry. This particular dogbone geometry uses a double fillet so to prevent any sharp corners. When modifying this particular piece I had to remove a significant chunk of material from the bottom left corner By widening the slot, it left a really small pinch point in the material which would have likely broken off after cutting. So I made the decision to remove the geometry to the left of the slot, however I left some small nub to help hold the parts together. </p>
<h2 id="vcarve">VCarve<a class="headerlink" href="#vcarve" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h2>
<p><img alt="" src="../../images/vcarve.jpg" />
I used Vcarve to generate the gcode for the piece. In this photo I&rsquo;m selecting the 1/4&rdquo; downcut tool, and making sure the feed and speed and rpm are appropriate for the material. I also can set the pass depth and stepover here. Since this is just a profile cut, I don&rsquo;t need to worry about step over dimensions. However, in the <a href="http://fabacademy.org/2019/labs/dassault/students/daniel-smithwick/assignments/week10/">Casting and Molding Week</a>, I did adjust these settings to get the precision of the slope.</p>
<h2 id="cnc-cutting">CNC Cutting<a class="headerlink" href="#cnc-cutting" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h2>
<p><img alt="" src="../../images/shopbot.jpg" />
Here is the Shopbot control interface. First, you need to manually jog the tool over to your 0,0. You do this with the blue Keypad arrows.</p>
<p><img alt="" src="../../images/sacrifice.jpg" />
Our sacrificial layer has been sacrificed!</p>
<p><img alt="" src="../../images/shopbot1.jpg" />
The 1&rdquo; foam cut quite well. I could probably speed up the feed rate.</p>
<p>I secured the parts to the table using wood screws.</p>
<h2 id="final-piece">Final Piece<a class="headerlink" href="#final-piece" title="Permanent link">&para;</a></h2>
<p><img alt="" src="../../images/cncpartdetail.jpg" />
Here&rsquo;s a detail of the dog bone design - a modified &lsquo;H&rsquo; section. I&rsquo;ve also added fillets to the all exterior corners to mimimize breakage.</p>
<p><img alt="" src="../../images/cncpart1test.jpg" />
Here&rsquo;s the full piece! It&rsquo;s about 30&rdquo; tall, 1&rdquo; thick. </p>
