 <h4> The RGB has 3 leds: Red, Green, Blue and they have a common anode. <a href="http://archive.fabacademy.org/fabacademy2017/fablabuae/students/154/"><b>Hashim</b></a> explained to me what common anode means and how it work. So you see in the image that the anode is (+) VCC and all RGB connected to the anode which means they are all conected to the VCC but from the other side they all connected to different pins which is (-) Ground, so you can turn them off separately.<br> 
     PWM (<b>0</b>= zero volt <b>255</b>= 5 volt) and here we can change the numbers between 0-255 regarding to what color you want. So the RGB will work in reverse way so for example if we give the LED 5V it will turn off because then the LED will connected to the ++ in both sides.</h4>
