    <div class="row"><div class="col-sm-12"><br><h3>STEPS PERFORMED:</h3></div></div>
    <div class="row">
            <div class="col-sm-12"><br><span style="color:#0B3C5D; font-weight: bold;"><strong>READING THE DATA SHEET</strong></span></div>
        </div> 
    <div class="row" id="row1">
            <div class="col-sm-12"><br>              
                
                <ul>
                    <li> My first step was to go through the tutorial provided in <a href="https://www.sparkfun.com/tutorials/223"> THIS LINK</a> to know more about what is an electronic component datasheet and what are the typical contents that can usually be found in a datasheet. Then I downloaded the ATTiny44 <a href="source_files/w9/doc8183.pdf"> DETAILED DTATSHEET</a> and <a href="source_files/w9/8183S.pdf"> SUMMARY DATASHEET</a>.<br> </li> 
                    <li> <strong> ELECTRONIC COMPONENT DATASHEET</strong> is simply the ultimate reference and instruction manuals for the electronic component. It explains exactly what a component does and how to use it.</li>
                    <li>Typical contents that I could find in the ATTiny44 datasheet were:  a summary of the partâ€™s function and features, Pinout functions and descriptions, absolute maximum ratings and recommended operating conditions and packaging and layout considerations.</li>
                    <li> The summary datasheet was 22 pages and the detailed datasheet was 286 pages, I realized that like any reference it was crucila to identify what I need to lookup and go to find it out instead of reading the full datasheet. I went first very quickly through the summary datasheet (i.e: the 22 pages) and I decided that I will definitely need to know: THE GENERAL FETAURES, THE PINOUTS and THE PIN DESCRIPTIONS. <br></li>
                 
               </ul>  
                 <ul>
                    <li> <STRONG>FEATURES:</STRONG> The ATTiny44 is high Performance, low Power 8-bit Microcontroller with advanced RISC architecture, I found the following very useful:</li>
                     <ul>
                        <li>2K/4K/8K Bytes of Flash Program Memory, 128/256/512 Bytes of Programmable EEPROM. 128/256/512 bytes SRAM.</li>
                         <li>12 general purpose I/O lines, 32 general purpose working registers and 8-channel 10-bit ADC.</li>
                         <li>Internal calibrated oscillator.</li>
                         <li>Operating voltage between 1.8 ~ 5.5V.</li>
                         <li>It has two kinds of ports: Port A pins are 8-bit bi-directional I/O port with internal pull-up resistors (pin names are from PA0 to PA7) and Port B pins are 4-bit bi-directional I/O port with internal pull-up resistors(pin names are from PB0 to PB3).</li>
                     </ul>
               </ul>  
                 <ul style="list-style-type: none;"> 
                         <li><img style="padding:1px; border:1px solid #1D2731;" class="img-fluid rounded mb-4" src="images/w9_datasheet_01.jpg" alt=""><br></li>

                <div class="alert alert-dark">
                    <strong>WHAT IS FTDI AND WHAT FTDI CABLE I USED?</strong><br> The FTDI cable is a USB to Serial (TTL level) converter which allows for a simple way to connect TTL interface devices to USB. The I/O pins of this FTDI cable are configured to operate at 5V. The FTDI cable is designed around an FT232RQ, which is housed in a USB A connector. The other side of the cable is terminated with 6-pin connector with the following pinout: RTS, RX, TX, 5V, CTS, GND (RTS is the green cable and GND is black). <a href="https://www.sparkfun.com/products/9718" class="alert-link">CLICK HERE</a> to see the FTDI cable I used.
